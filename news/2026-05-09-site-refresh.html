Site Update

Website Refresh & OC 2.0 Guide

A sharper faction hub, clearer member resources, and a new guide built to help every member join Organized Crimes 2.0 with confidence.

Back to News

Published 09 May 2026 — Privé Cartel Council

The Privé Cartel website has received a focused quality pass aimed at making the hub easier to use, more professional, and more useful for day-to-day faction life. The work tightens the homepage, improves shared navigation and footer presentation, and introduces a full new-member guide for Organized Crimes 2.0.

New OC 2.0 Guide

A complete walkthrough covering slot selection, timers, items, readiness checks, payouts, mistakes and member expectations.

Cleaner Homepage

The live chain panel was retired and replaced with a clearer operations snapshot that better reflects current faction priorities.

Professional Footer

The plain footer has been replaced with a structured site footer, quick resources, council details and member links.

What Changed

Organized Crimes 2.0 guide added New members now have one clear page explaining how to join scenarios without delaying or risking the operation.
Guides hub updated The OC 2.0 guide is now a first-class card in the faction knowledge hub and appears in the shared footer resources.
Rules page linked The OC rules section now points directly to the guide for slot selection, item checks, timers and payout expectations.
Footer rebuilt Every page now has a stronger footer with navigation groups, resources, Discord access and council contact.
Homepage improved The old Current Chain live panel and associated API polling were removed, reducing clutter and stale live-status behavior.
Accessibility and reliability improved Navigation, focus states, reduced-motion handling and shared link behavior were tightened across the site.

Why It Matters

The website is no longer just a noticeboard. It is becoming the faction's operating manual: a place where new members can learn expectations quickly, active members can find resources without digging through chat, and everyone can rely on consistent guidance.

The new Organized Crimes 2.0 guide is the most important part of this release. OC participation depends on small details: joining the right slot, checking required tools and materials, watching the timer, and being in Torn when the operation initiates. The guide turns those details into a simple process members can follow.

Next Step For Members

Read the OC 2.0 guide before joining your next scenario. If your role score, timer, or item requirement looks questionable, ask before joining.
